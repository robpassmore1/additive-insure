# Broker Talk Track
## Additive Insure — Version 1.1 Controlled Demonstrator

This document outlines the specific narrative and discussion points when presenting the Additive Insure Version 1.1 Controlled Demonstrator to **Brokers**. It focuses on client relationship value, risk differentiation, and renewal negotiations.

---

## Broker Presentation Focus

Brokers care about **client retention, market placement, and proving client risk quality**. Your presentation should emphasize how Additive Insure helps brokers differentiate their clients' risks in a hard market, providing tangible evidence to secure capacity.

---

## Talking Points by Journey Stage

### 1. Site Engagement & Visibility (Site Event View - IDLE)
* **Goal:** Show how the broker's client gains control over their risk exposure.
* **Key Points:**
  * Direct attention to the **£50m Property / £75m BI exposure**. Explain that clients often feel powerless against rising flood risks.
  * Highlight the **survey-informed pathway**: this represents an active partnership between the broker, the client, and Additive to physically map and understand how water behaves on site.
  * Show that the client is not just buying a paper policy; they are gaining an active risk-monitoring system.

---

### 2. Operational Action (ALERT State)
* **Goal:** Showcase the client's active role in risk reduction.
* **Key Points:**
  * Highlight the **Mark Action Complete** interaction.
  * Explain that this logs the client's physical effort in real-time. It proves that the client is an "active risk manager," not a passive policyholder.
  * Point out that the 120-minute deployment window is realistic and manageable for the client's onsite facilities team.

---

### 3. The "Meeting Moment" & Avoided Loss (RESOLVED State)
* **Screen:** Underwriter / Broker View
* **Goal:** Position the avoided loss as the broker's key renewal lever.
* **Key Points:**
  * **£3,000,000 Probable Avoided Retained Loss:** This is the core number the broker takes to the market. Emphasize that by proving the client successfully avoided a £3 million loss within their excess, the broker can argue that the client is a superior risk.
  * Explain that this data changes the renewal conversation: *"Instead of arguing about generic flood maps, the broker can present hard evidence of the client's actual mitigation performance."*
  * **Evidence Gaps:** Point out that knowing the gaps (e.g., *no backup power*) allows the broker to consultatively advise the client on where to invest capital to further improve their risk rating.

---

### 4. The Evidence Summary & Export
* **Screen:** Evidence Summary
* **Goal:** Demonstrate the final deliverable that the broker uses.
* **Key Points:**
  * Click the **Print / Export** button to display the clean, print-ready document.
  * Explain that this PDF/printout is the "renewal pack." The broker attaches this report directly to the market submission.
  * It provides the underwriter with a complete audit trail of the event, the action, the sensor readings, and the avoided loss calculations—backed by clear disclaimers that maintain credibility.

---

## Critical Governance Cautions for Brokers

When discussing this with brokers, you must reinforce the following constraints:
1. **No Guaranteed Premium Discounts:** Version 1.1 cannot guarantee premium rate reductions. Actuarial models must be developed in future versions.
2. **Not a Claims Settling Tool:** This evidence pack is for risk-quality demonstration and cannot be used as legally binding claims proof.
3. **Simulated Telemetry:** Remind brokers that all sensors and water levels shown are simulated conceptual models, not live IoT feeds.
