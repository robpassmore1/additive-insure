# Investor & Strategic Partner Talk Track
## Additive Insure — Version 1.1 Controlled Demonstrator

This document outlines the specific narrative and discussion points when presenting the Additive Insure Version 1.1 Controlled Demonstrator to **Investors and Strategic Partners**. It focuses on commercial vision, market opportunity, and scalable product architecture.

---

## Investor & Partner Presentation Focus

Investors and strategic partners care about **business model viability, scalability, market differentiation, and product roadmap execution**. Your presentation should highlight how Additive Insure disrupts traditional commercial property insurance by introducing a dynamic, performance-based underwriting model.

---

## Talking Points by Journey Stage

### 1. The Market Problem (Site Event View - IDLE)
* **Goal:** Position Additive Insure as the solution to commercial flood under-insurance.
* **Key Points:**
  * Point out the **£50m Property / £75m BI exposure** and **£4m Flood Excess**.
  * Explain the commercial insurance crisis: *"As climate risk escalates, insurers are raising excesses to £4M+ or withdrawing flood coverage entirely. This leaves commercial buyers carrying massive, unhedged balance sheet risk."*
  * Explain that Additive Insure is designed specifically to address this retained risk window, creating a new class of risk-transfer product.

---

### 2. Physical-to-Financial Translation (ALERT & RESOLVED States)
* **Goal:** Demonstrate the proprietary value of verifying physical actions.
* **Key Points:**
  * Walk through the **Watch/Action Alert** and the logging of action completion.
  * Explain that the core intellectual property of Additive is the *verifiable audit trail*. By capturing sensor heights and human actions in the Impact Ledger, we prove causality.
  * Contrast this with traditional insurance: *"Traditional insurers write a policy and hope for the best. Additive creates an active, closed-loop system where physical risk-reduction is tracked, verified, and valued."*

---

### 3. Commercial Value & The "Meeting Moment"
* **Screen:** Underwriter / Broker View
* **Goal:** Prove the value created for the commercial ecosystem.
* **Key Points:**
  * **£3,000,000 Probable Avoided Retained Loss:** This represents direct value creation. Highlight that by avoiding this loss, the client protects their balance sheet, the broker retains a happy client, and the underwriter avoids a potential excess-breaching claim.
  * Explain that this creates a triple-win: the client gets risk visibility, the broker gets a powerful retention tool, and the underwriter gets a better-managed risk portfolio.

---

### 4. Portfolio Scale (Portfolio Teaser)
* **Screen:** Portfolio Teaser
* **Goal:** Show how the system scales across thousands of sites.
* **Key Points:**
  * Walk through the **4-row Portfolio Register**. Explain that while we focus on the FMCG hero site, the backend database structure is designed to aggregate hundreds of commercial properties.
  * Show how varying confidence levels (*Live, Partial, Stub*) allow us to manage a mixed portfolio, providing a clear path to onboard new clients via a phased sensor roll-out.

---

## The Strategic Roadmap (Version 1.1 to 1.2 and Beyond)

Conclude the investor presentation by highlighting our disciplined product development process:
* **Version 1.1 (Today):** A fully realized, meeting-ready demonstrator used to secure broker partnerships and pilot commitments.
* **Version 1.2 (Next):** Incorporating feedback from these pilot partners to harden the narrative and finalize the telemetry integration design.
* **Long-term Vision:** Unlocking parametric and hybrid insurance structures. By having verified physical proof of mitigation, we can underwrite policies that pay out faster and cost less.

---

## Critical Governance Cautions for Partners

To maintain professional credibility, explicitly note the following:
* **No Live Forecasting Yet:** Telemetry is fully simulated in this version to show the workflow.
* **Regulatory Compliance:** Underwriting and premium calculation are conceptual; full compliance and actuarial validation are scheduled for future development phases.
