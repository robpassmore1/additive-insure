# Underwriter Talk Track
## Additive Insure — Version 1.1 Controlled Demonstrator

This document outlines the specific narrative and discussion points when presenting the Additive Insure Version 1.1 Controlled Demonstrator to **Underwriters**. It focuses on risk visibility, evidence quality, and commercial exposure management.

---

## Underwriter Presentation Focus

Underwriters care about **exposure, verification, and contract structure**. Your presentation should emphasize how Additive Insure provides verifiable proof of risk mitigation, helping to substantiate risk quality.

---

## Talking Points by Journey Stage

### 1. Initial Risk Profile (Site Event View - IDLE)
* **Goal:** Establish the boundary between insured risk and the policyholder's retained risk.
* **Key Points:**
  * Direct attention to the **Flood Excess (£4,000,000)** and **Daily Downtime Cost (£350,000)**.
  * Highlight that the demonstrator focuses on losses that fall *within* the policyholder's excess (retained risk).
  * Emphasize the **survey-informed flood pathway**: the pathway is a pre-calculated model of how water enters the site, based on engineering surveys.

---

### 2. Alert & Mitigation Window (ALERT State)
* **Goal:** Demonstrate the predictability and operational buffer of the risk.
* **Key Points:**
  * Point out the **P50 Lead Time (240 mins)** vs. the **Minimum Required Deployment Time (120 mins)**.
  * Explain that this 2-hour buffer gives underwriters confidence that the site team has sufficient time to respond before water reaches critical vulnerabilities.
  * State clearly: *"We do not predict the weather; we measure the physical buffer time available to execute verified risk-reduction actions."*

---

### 3. The "Meeting Moment" & Avoided Loss (RESOLVED State)
* **Screen:** Underwriter / Broker View
* **Goal:** Walk through the financial impact and evidence auditing.
* **Key Points:**
  * **£3,000,000 Probable Avoided Retained Loss:** This represents the calculated exposure that was mitigated by the barriers. It is a *retained* loss reduction (within the £4m excess), meaning it directly protects the client's balance sheet and reduces the likelihood of the loss expanding to breach the £4m policy excess.
  * **Evidence Gaps Panel:** Explicitly point out the "Evidence Gaps" section (e.g., *no secondary backup sensor*, *unverified mobile pump status*). Explain that this transparency allows underwriters to apply appropriate risk credits based on evidence completeness rather than assumptions.
  * **Causality & Confidence Ratings:** Walk through the ratings (*Hydrological Causality: Probable*, *Evidence Confidence: Probable avoided loss*). Explain that these qualifiers ensure the financial figures are never presented as absolute guarantees.

---

### 4. The Contrast Scenario (IDLE/Reset State)
* **Goal:** Contrast active risk management with passive exposure.
* **Key Points:**
  * Emphasize the **£3,500,000 Unmitigated Probable Retained Exposure**.
  * Explain that if no action is logged, the underwriter sees the full exposure. The risk profile does not improve. This ensures that premium credits or risk assessments are tied strictly to *verified performance* rather than promise.

---

## Critical Governance Cautions for Underwriters

When discussing this with underwriters, you must reinforce the following constraints:
1. **Not for Premium Pricing:** Version 1.1 commercial metrics are mock/simulated values. They cannot be used to price policies or calculate actual premium reductions today.
2. **Not for Binding/Underwriting Acceptance:** This is a demonstration of the *workflow and data model*. It is not a live underwriting portal.
3. **Evidence-Qualified:** Avoided loss is "evidence-qualified" (probable impact based on sensor and action logs), not mathematical certainty.
